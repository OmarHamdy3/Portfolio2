Understanding the scope and objectives of the analysis

Brief Project Idea

This project analyzes a superstore's sales data to identify profit optimization opportunities, understand customer behavior patterns, and provide actionable business intelligence through interactive Excel dashboards.

Main Objective

  • Increase Profitability: Identify low-margin products and optimize pricing strategies
  • Reduce Losses: Analyze discount impact on profit margins
  • Improve Inventory: Optimize stock based on demand patterns
  • Enhance Efficiency: Automate reporting and reduce manual work

Critical challenges requiring data-driven solutions

  • Profit Decline Despite Sales Growth: Sales increased 15% YoY but profit margins dropped from 14% to 11% due to excessive discounting.

  • Inventory Inefficiencies: 15% of SKUs overstocked while 20% of bestsellers frequently face stockouts.
  • Manual Reporting Delays: Management receives reports 2-3 weeks after month-end, preventing timely decisions.

8. Key Business Insights

Data-driven discoveries with business implications

Product Concentration

20% of products generate 85% of total profit. Technology category dominates despite lower volume.

Discount Trap

Discounts >20% consistently lead to negative profits, especially in Furniture category where 35% of sales are discounted.

Regional Disparity

Central region has high sales but low profits due to 2x higher shipping costs and excessive discounting.

Seasonal Patterns

Q4 sales spike 85% requires 3-month inventory lead time. Current stockouts cost an estimated $120K annually.

Furniture Problem

Tables and Bookcases consistently lose money. 40% of Furniture sub-categories operate at negative margins.

Customer Value

Corporate customers have 2.5x higher lifetime value but receive 15% higher discounts than other segments.

Shipping Impact

Same-day shipping reduces profit margin by 8% compared to Standard Class. Only profitable for high-value orders >$1,000.

Growth Opportunity

West region model (higher prices, lower discounts) could increase Central region profit by 40% if replicated.

12. Tools & Technologies

Key DAX Measures

Total Accidents = COUNT(Accidents[Accident ID])
Severity Index = 
    SUMX(
        Accidents,
        Accidents[Fatal] * 10 + 
        Accidents[Serious] * 3 + 
        Accidents[Slight] * 1
    )
Risk Score = DIVIDE([Severity Index], [Total Accidents], 0)
Peak Hour Risk = 
CALCULATE(
    [Total Accidents],
    Accidents[Hour] >= 17 && Accidents[Hour] <= 19,
    Accidents[DayOfWeek] = "Friday"
)
